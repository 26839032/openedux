Behaviorist Learning Theory

Emphasizes the connection between stimulus and response, shaping learning behaviors through reinforcement and punishment, focusing on observable external behavioral changes.

Cognitivist Learning Theory

Focuses on learners' internal psychological processes and cognitive structures, emphasizing information processing, memory, problem-solving, and active knowledge construction.

Bloom's Mastery Learning Theory

Believes that with sufficient time and appropriate instruction, the vast majority of students can master any learning content, emphasizing personalization and feedback.
