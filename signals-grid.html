How Grid Score Works

Grid Score evaluates an ETF’s suitability for swing/grid trading over a 30–90 day horizon. Three dimensions are scored independently, then combined.

GridScore = Volatility × 0.30 + Trend × 0.20 + Range × 0.20 + WaterLevel × 0.15 + MA250 × 0.15

Volatility (30%)

20-day annualized historical volatility. The highest weight — grid profits come from movement, not from being cheap. 15%–40% is ideal.

Trend (20%)

MA20 vs MA60 relationship. Sideways markets score highest. Strong single-direction trends are penalized — grids work best in ranges.

Range / Oscillation (20%)

How many times the price crossed above or below MA20 in the past 90 days. More crossovers = more oscillation = better grid environment. The real grid paradise is ↖↘↖↘, not ↗↗↗↗.

Water Level (15%)

Where the current price sits within the 52-week range. 20%–60% is ideal. Downgraded — position matters less than movement for grid trading.

Distance to MA250 (15%)

How far the price has drifted from its 250-day moving average. Closer is better — mean reversion is the grid trader’s gravity.
